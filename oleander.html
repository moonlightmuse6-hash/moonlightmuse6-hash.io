Oleander: The Killer Behind the Blooms

With its vibrant clusters of pink, white, or red blossoms, oleander (Nerium oleander) is a favorite in warm climates—but this drought-resistant stunner is one of the most poisonous plants on Earth. Every part of it, contains cardiac glycosides, toxins that can stop a human heart in hours. Ingesting a leaf may cause violent vomiting, irregular heartbeat, seizures, and death. Even inhaling smoke from burning oleander or using its twigs as skewers has proven fatal. A popular but perilous ornamental, it’s the ultimate "look, don’t touch" plant—unless you fancy playing Russian roulette with your salad.
