Iris: Elegant but Edgy

With their regal blooms and sword-like leaves, irises add striking beauty to gardens, but hidden in their rhizomes lies a toxic surprise. These plants contain irisin, iridin, and other irritating compounds that can turn a curious nibble into a nasty experience. Symptoms range from mouth burning and stomach cramps to vomiting and diarrhea, unpleasant but rarely deadly. The real risk? Mistaking their roots for edible look-alikes (like sweet flag). While not the most dangerous plant, they’re a reminder: not all that’s gorgeous is good to taste.
