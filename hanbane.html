Henbane: The Devil’s Herbe

With its foul-smelling, yellow-throated blooms and hairy leaves, henbane (Hyoscyamus niger) looks as sinister as its reputation. This ancient poison packs tropane alkaloids,the same toxins found in deadly nightshade. Even small doses cause violent hallucinations, convulsions, and paralysis; larger amounts can stop breathing. Historically used in witches’ brews and assassins’ potions, it’s now a forbidden fascination in gardens. Handle with extreme caution, this plant’s legacy is as toxic as its roots.
