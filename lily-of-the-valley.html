Lily of the Valley: A Fragrant Foe

Delicate and sweetly scented, lily of the valley (Convallaria majalis) charms with its dainty white bells—but don’t let its innocence fool you. This spring favorite packs cardiac glycosides, toxins that can cause lethal heart rhythms if ingested. Even a small bite leads to nausea, vomiting, dizziness, and irregular heartbeat. Its lookalike leaves and red berries tempt foragers, but this plant is no gentle woodland friend. Perfect for bouquets, but keep it far from kids and pets—its beauty is truly skin-deep
